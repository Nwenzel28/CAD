About Orangineering

A structured learning platform built for intermediate CAD users who want to think and work like engineers, not just operate software.

Built by engineers, for engineers who want to go further.

There's a frustrating gap in how CAD is taught. Tutorials teach you the software. Classes teach you theory. But nobody shows you what happens in between — the decisions, the failures, the constraints that define real engineering work.

Orangineering exists to fill that gap. It's a structured learning platform built around the premise that intermediate engineers learn best from real-world examples, documented honestly — including the parts that didn't work.

Every module connects to a case study. Every case study references the resources that powered it. The platform is designed to work as a connected learning journey, not a pile of isolated lessons.

The name is a combination of Open + Engineering — with a nod to orange, the color of energy, construction, and things being built. If it makes you smile, that's intentional.

Engineering isn't just knowing the tools. It's knowing which question to ask when something doesn't work — and having enough context to answer it.

— Orangineering Design Principle
⚙
Context Over Commands

We teach the why behind every design decision, not just the how-to.

🔁
Failure is Part of the Process

Every case study includes what broke, why, and what changed — because that's where the learning actually happens.

📐
Structured, but Not Rigid

Modules build on each other, but you can jump to what you need. Your learning path should fit your project.
